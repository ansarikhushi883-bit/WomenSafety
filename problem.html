The Challenge
Problem Statement
Women often face unsafe situations while traveling, working late hours, or living alone. Existing safety mechanisms suffer from critical gaps that put lives at risk every day.

"Women need safety solutions that are instant, reliable, and accessible — not systems that fail at the moment they're needed most."

— SafeHer Platform Vision Statement
⏱️

Delayed Emergency Access

Current systems create dangerous delays when women need immediate assistance in unsafe situations. Every second of delay increases risk.

📡

No Responder Coordination

Lack of coordination among nearby responders results in slow, disorganized emergency response and duplicated efforts.

📞

Manual Phone Dependence

Reliance on manual phone calls is slow and unreliable in panic situations where physical actions are difficult and frightening.

🏢

No Centralized Platform

No unified system exists to centralize emergency assistance requests, assign responders, and track incidents end-to-end.

📍

Limited Location Tracking

Without real-time GPS sharing, responders cannot efficiently locate and navigate to women in distress — wasting critical time.

🔍

Lack of Awareness

Many women are unaware of nearby safe zones, support teams, or verified volunteers who could assist during emergencies.
